# Composition — arranging weight on the page

Layout (`layout.md`) handles structure and the grid; composition handles where the eye goes and why. A page can be perfectly gridded and still flat. This module is about deliberate visual weight.

## One focal point per view

Each viewport-height of the page has **one** clear first landing — the thing the eye hits first. Everything else is secondary or tertiary. Two equal-weight focal points fight and the page reads as noise. Establish weight through size, contrast, isolation (whitespace around a thing makes it loud), or color — not by making everything big.

## Direct the eye path

Decide the intended reading order and build the page to enforce it: scale steps down through the hierarchy, alignment creates lines the eye follows, a deliberate diagonal or Z/F path for scannable content, leading lines (an arrow, an image's gaze direction, a gradient) point at the next beat. The CTA sits where the path lands, not wherever there's room.

## Figure-ground and contrast

The subject must separate cleanly from its background — via tone, a containing surface, a subtle scrim under text on imagery, or depth (`depth.md`). Text over photos/video always gets a legibility treatment (scrim, gradient, or a solid panel); never raw text on a busy image.

## Tension over symmetry

Perfect symmetry reads as static and "default". Prefer:

- **Asymmetric balance** — a large element offset by several small ones, weighted around an optical (not geometric) center.
- **The rule of odds** — groups of 3/5 feel more natural than 2/4.
- **Intentional negative space** — emptiness is a compositional element, not wasted room; the most confident layouts leave more air than feels safe.
- **A broken edge** — one element that escapes the grid (overlap, bleed) creates the focal tension a clean grid lacks. (Used deliberately; see `layout.md` broken-grid.)

## Density rhythm

Across a page, vary density: a dense section followed by a calm one creates pace. A flat run of equal-density, equal-height sections is the "brochure" failure the QA gate already flags for landings.

## Status

One focal point per view, a deliberate eye path, clean figure-ground, asymmetric balance, density rhythm: **MUSTHAVE-DEFAULT**. Composition is judged at the QA gate as "does the eye have an obvious first landing and a path" — now backed by the rendered screenshot.
