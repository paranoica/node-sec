# Layout

## The strength ladder

Layout composition climbs a ladder of force:

`left edge → 12-column grid → asymmetry → overlap`

- **Level 0 — left edge.** Everything aligned to the left margin. Calm, safe, readable.
- **Level 1 — 12-column grid.** Structured placement, varied column spans.
- **Level 2–3 — asymmetry.** Elements deliberately off-balance; weight distributed unevenly; diagonal flow.
- **Level 4–5 — overlap.** Elements break their cells, cross each other, edge-to-edge bleeds, grid-breaking.

## Mode mapping

- **Clean** — layout level 0–1.
- **Statement** — layout level 2–5.

## Air is the default

Generous negative space is the default posture. Density is a deliberate choice (data-dense family, dashboards), confirmed in the survey — not a fallback.

## Intensity by hierarchy

Even in Statement, not every section is at level 5. The hero may be overlap-heavy; an ordinary content section sits calmer at level 1–2. The mode sets the ceiling; hierarchy distributes within it.

## Photography in layout

Overlap and asymmetry apply to photos too — a photo can layer, bleed past a section edge, carry elements on top. A flat lone rectangle of a photo in the middle of a section is the most boring option. See `principles/photography.md`.

## Broken / anti-grid (deliberate, with guardrails)

A deliberately broken grid — elements that overlap, bleed off-edge, or sit off the column rhythm — creates the human, authored tension a perfect grid lacks. Rules so it reads as intent, not breakage:
- Break **from** an established grid; the underlying order must be felt for the break to register. Random placement just looks broken.
- Break **one or few** elements, not everything — the focal/overlap moment is the point.
- **Never break readability**: body text keeps its measure and alignment; only display elements, images, and accents break.
- **Domain fit** (`aesthetic-families.md`): anti-grid suits creative/editorial/fashion; it erodes trust on finance/healthcare/enterprise. Guard before using.

## Status

- The strength ladder and air-as-default: **MUSTHAVE-BASE** (the *ladder* is always the model; which rung is chosen depends on mode).
- Levels 2–5 (asymmetry, overlap): **STATEMENT**.
