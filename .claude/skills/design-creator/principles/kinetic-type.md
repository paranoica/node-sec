# Kinetic Typography

Text that moves on its own. Kinetic text is almost always **decorative** — genuinely important information must never be made to move, chase, or assemble. The one exception is the word-by-word heading reveal (there the text is static in its final state; only the appearance moves).

## The techniques

- **Marquee** — large word-band scrolling horizontally; fills wide empty strips between sections. Decorative text only. Slow. Duplicate the band for seamlessness.
- **Text on a circle** — decorative badge/emblem around an icon or CTA. Short repeating phrase.
- **Word-by-word reveal** — heading words rise out of a mask with a stagger. Elegant, not overused. Good for hero.
- **Typewriter** — phrase typed letter by letter, cycling tips. Works but overused — dose it. First frame must be real text in the markup (SEO / accessibility).
- **Scroll-velocity reaction** — text deforms with scroll speed. Strong award technique; STATEMENT; mandatory `prefers-reduced-motion`; large headings only.

## Status

- Marquee, text on a circle, word-by-word reveal: **SITUATIONAL** (free to use, proposed in survey).
- Typewriter: **SITUATIONAL**, dosed.
- Scroll-velocity reaction: **STATEMENT**, sometimes worth a survey question.
