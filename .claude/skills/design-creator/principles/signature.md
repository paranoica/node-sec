# Signature — the thing that makes it *this* brand

Anti-slop removes the generic; signature adds the specific. A signature is one repeated, ownable idea that recurs across the site so the whole thing feels authored by one hand rather than assembled from good parts.

## Pick one signature device (not five)

Choose a single motif and carry it everywhere:

- **A graphic motif** — a recurring shape, a custom corner treatment, a line/connector style, a grain, a halftone, a specific border idiom. It appears in the logo lockup, section dividers, hover states, the favicon.
- **A motion fingerprint** — one custom `cubic-bezier` used for every transition on the site, so motion *feels* consistent (a slightly overshooting ease, a heavy decelerate). One curve, applied everywhere, is more recognizable than five tasteful defaults.
- **A type signature** — one accent face or one always-the-same treatment of the key word (the same slash, underline-draw, or color on every emphasized term).
- **A color strike** — the accent always arriving the same way (always as a highlight behind a word, never as a fill).

## Carry it through transitions

Use **shared-element transitions** so the signature persists *between* states and pages — a card's image that morphs into the detail hero, an element that animates from list to page. Native cross-document View Transitions make this possible without an SPA (`micro-mechanics.md`). The signature isn't only static marks; it's how things move from one place to the next.

## Restraint

One signature, applied consistently, beats a pile of clever touches. If a second "signature" idea appears, it's decoration — demote it. The test: could someone recognize a cropped, logo-less screenshot as this brand? If not, the signature isn't doing its job.

## Status

A single chosen signature device carried across surfaces and transitions: **MUSTHAVE-DEFAULT** for Statement and for any branded project; **SITUATIONAL** for plain app surfaces where neutrality is the point.
