# Reference: Clean-mode exemplar

A worked example of a coherent Clean-mode landing. Illustrates how the principles compose when the mode is Clean. Not a template to copy verbatim — a demonstration of the reasoning.

## Aesthetic family
Editorial minimalism.

## Color (ladder)
- Background: warm near-white cream, not pure white.
- Surface: a small step off the background.
- Border: one more small step.
- Secondary text: a warm mid-gray.
- Primary text: a warm near-black, not `#000`.
- Accent: one muted color (e.g. a considered clay or teal), ~10% of the screen — on the CTA, links, one underlined word.

## Typography
- Hero: scale + tight line-height + slight negative tracking — a restrained 3-technique subset (Clean does not use the full 7).
- One characterful display face for the headline, one workhorse grotesque for body.
- Body: line-height 1.6, tracking 0.
- Cyrillic checked on both faces.

## Layout
- Level 0–1: left-edge alignment, a 12-column grid.
- Generous air throughout.

## Motion
- MUSTHAVE-BASE + MUSTHAVE-DEFAULT only: scroll-reveal entrance, list stagger, number counter, link underline-slide, crisp 120–180ms responses.
- No Statement techniques.

## Storytelling
- Scroll-reveal on every section, a number counter on stats, calm rhythm (dense → air → dense).

## Themes
Two themes, smooth color cross-fade transition.

## Why it works
Nothing shouts; everything is considered. The "premium" comes from the flawless neutral ladder, one disciplined accent, and restrained typography technique — not from effects. This is the Vercel/Linear lineage executed with care.
