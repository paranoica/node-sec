# Pattern: Testimonials

This file gives Claude what to assemble, not what to copy.

## 1. Purpose
Build trust through other people's voices — turn "they claim" into "others confirm".

## 2. Block composition
**Required:** a section heading, testimonial units (each: a quote, an author name, an author role/company).
**Optional:** an author photo or initials-fallback, a company logo, a rating, a metric/result, a logo wall, a carousel.

## 3. Order logic
Heading → testimonial units. Lead with the strongest, most specific quote. A concrete result ("cut onboarding to 2 days") outweighs a vague compliment — order by specificity, not by length.

## 4. Variants by mode
- **Clean** — an even grid or a calm single-quote block.
- **Statement** — varied unit sizes, one quote oversized as a statement, marquee logo wall, scroll-reveal stagger.

## 5. Technique bindings
- Author photos follow `principles/photography.md` (fixed ratio, object-fit cover, real photos / initials).
- Stagger on entrance (`principles/motion.md`).
- A logo marquee is a kinetic-type option (`principles/kinetic-type.md`).
- A carousel obeys overlay/transition smoothness (`principles/micro-mechanics.md`).

## 6. Typical mistakes
- Stock-photo faces — instantly fake.
- Vague quotes with no specifics or attribution.
- Author photos at mismatched aspect ratios tearing the grid.

## 7. The hook in this section
Carry the site's hook through this section too, not just the hero (`principles/concept.md`): state per build how it advances the one central idea, or how it stays deliberately quiet so the hook lands elsewhere.
